## Preface

I'm not a gear fanatic. A great video can be made with any decent camera — what makes the difference is light, movement, and editing. With that said, here's what I use.

## Main camera

My main choice has stayed stable over the last two years. I prefer a camera I know well rather than chasing the latest release. Familiarity with the menus, the limitations, and the behaviour in difficult situations is worth more than any spec sheet.

## Audio

> Audio is 50% of video. Often more.

That's why I invest as much in it as in the camera. A shotgun microphone for outdoor shoots, a wireless lavalier for interviews, a portable recorder as backup.

## Post-production

Editing on DaVinci Resolve. Free, powerful, and the colour correction is unbeatable at this price point (which is zero).

## What I've dropped

- **Motorised gimbal**: great in theory, awkward in daily practice. Handheld movement, when controlled, has a life that the gimbal tends to flatten.
- **Drone**: too much bureaucracy for the occasional use I made of it.

---

The rule I follow: if a piece of kit doesn't come out with me on shoots, there's no point buying it.
