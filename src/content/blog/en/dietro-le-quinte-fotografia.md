## The problem of choice

Every time I come back from a photography session I find myself with hundreds of shots. The temptation is to keep them all, but real photography starts right here: in the selection.

## My three-step process

### 1. The first cull

I import everything into Lightroom and do a quick first pass. I immediately discard:

- Out-of-focus shots
- Unintentional blur
- Near-identical duplicates

Already at this stage I cut 60–70% of the shots.

### 2. The emotional selection

In the second pass I slow down. I no longer look at technique — I look at the story. I ask myself: *does this shot say something?* Does it have a tension, a precise moment, something that cannot be repeated?

Here I reduce further to 10–15% of the total.

### 3. Post-production

Only on the surviving shots do I work in post. I prefer a light touch: exposure correction, white balance, a few curve adjustments. Rarely more than five minutes per image.

---

The result is often a selection of 10–20 photos from a session of 300+ shots. Few, but right.
